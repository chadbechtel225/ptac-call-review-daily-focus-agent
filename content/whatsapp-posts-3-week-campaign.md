# 3-Week WhatsApp Call Training Campaign

**Own the First 10 Seconds. Control the Last 30 Seconds.**

# Week 1: Required Greeting + Professional First Impression

## Monday Focus: The Greeting Is the Standard

Team, this week our focus is the required answering procedure.

Every call starts this way:

“Thank you for choosing Precision Tune Auto Care, my name is [employee’s name], who do I have the pleasure of speaking with?”

That greeting is not optional. Everyone has been trained. Everyone knows the expectation. Now we execute.

That opening thanks the customer, confirms they reached Precision Tune Auto Care, identifies who they are speaking with, and gets the customer’s name so the relationship starts properly.

Today’s challenge: every call starts with the required greeting exactly.

Automotive Hospitality starts before the vehicle ever enters the bay.

---

## Tuesday Focus: Say It Clearly

Team, today’s focus is clarity.

The required greeting only works if the customer can actually understand it.

Do not mumble it. Do not rush it. Do not say it like you are trying to survive jury duty.

Say it clearly:

“Thank you for choosing Precision Tune Auto Care, my name is [employee’s name], who do I have the pleasure of speaking with?”

The customer should immediately know they reached the right place and are speaking with someone professional.

Today’s challenge: slow down and say the full greeting clearly every time.

Professionalism is a practiced standard.

---

## Wednesday Focus: Your Name Matters

Team, today’s focus is ownership.

“My name is [employee’s name]…”

That part matters. Customers trust people, not buildings. When you give your name, you create accountability and connection.

Do not skip it. Do not mumble it. Do not treat it like filler.

Say your name like you are proud to serve the customer.

Today’s challenge: every call includes your name clearly and confidently.

Customers should feel welcomed, heard, guided, and confident.

---

## Thursday Focus: Get and Use the Customer’s Name

Team, today’s focus is the final part of the greeting:

“Who do I have the pleasure of speaking with?”

That question changes the call. It turns a transaction into a relationship.

Once you have the customer’s name, use it naturally during the call.

People want to be treated like people before they are treated like repair orders.

Today’s challenge: get the customer’s name and use it at least once during the conversation.

---

## Friday Focus: Greeting Audit

Team, today we audit the first 10 seconds.

Ask yourself:

- Did I say “Thank you for choosing Precision Tune Auto Care”?
- Did I say “my name is…”?
- Did I ask, “Who do I have the pleasure of speaking with?”
- Did I sound clear, warm, and professional?

If the answer is no, fix it on the next call.

The greeting is not difficult. The only thing required is discipline.

Today’s challenge: audit yourself and correct the next call immediately.

---

# Week 2: Active Listening + Clarifying Questions

## Monday Focus: Let the Customer Finish

Team, this week we focus on active listening.

Today’s rule: do not interrupt the customer.

Let them explain the concern fully before jumping in with answers. When we interrupt, we may think we are being efficient, but the customer hears impatience.

A two-second pause before responding can change the entire feel of the call.

Today’s challenge: let every customer finish their sentence before you respond.

Patience sells.

---

## Tuesday Focus: Ask Better Questions

Team, today’s focus is clarifying questions.

A good advisor does not guess. A good advisor investigates.

Use questions like:

- “When did you first notice it?”
- “Does it happen all the time or only sometimes?”
- “Are there any lights on the dash?”
- “Have you had this looked at before?”

Better questions create better inspections, better estimates, and better trust.

Today’s challenge: ask at least two clarifying questions before recommending the next step.

---

## Wednesday Focus: Paraphrase the Concern

Team, today’s focus is repeating back what the customer told you.

Example:

“So just to make sure I understand, the vehicle is overheating mostly at idle, and you noticed it yesterday. Correct?”

That one habit builds trust fast. It shows the customer you listened and it reduces confusion later.

Today’s challenge: paraphrase the customer’s concern before moving to the appointment or inspection.

---

## Thursday Focus: Listen for the Real Need

Team, today’s focus is listening beneath the words.

Sometimes the customer says, “How much is a diagnostic?”

What they may really mean is, “Can I trust you not to waste my money?”

Sometimes they say, “Can you just look at it?”

What they may really mean is, “I need help, but I am nervous about cost.”

Do not just answer the words. Understand the concern behind the words.

Today’s challenge: identify what the customer is really worried about before closing the call.

---

## Friday Focus: Patience Wins Trust

Team, today’s reminder: patience sells.

Rushing makes us sound careless. Listening makes us sound professional.

Customers are not an interruption to the work. Customers are the reason the work exists.

Today’s challenge: slow down, listen fully, and make the customer feel heard before you make them feel scheduled.

---

# Week 3: Clarity, Warmth, and Strong Call Closure

## Monday Focus: Say It Cleaner

Team, this week we focus on clarity.

Customers do not need rambling. They need direction.

Use this structure:

Acknowledge. Clarify. Recommend. Confirm.

Example:

“Absolutely, I understand. Since the vehicle is overheating, the best next step is to inspect the cooling system and verify the actual cause. We can get that started today. What time works best?”

Clean. Clear. Confident.

Today’s challenge: no rambling. Lead the customer.

---

## Tuesday Focus: Warmth Is Not Optional

Team, today’s focus is tone.

You can say the right words and still sound like you would rather be folding laundry in a thunderstorm.

The customer should hear that we are glad they called.

Smile before answering. Sit up. Speak clearly. Put energy in your voice.

Hospitality is not just what we say. It is how the customer feels when we say it.

Today’s challenge: make every customer feel welcomed, not processed.

---

## Wednesday Focus: Replace Weak Words

Team, today’s focus is removing weak language.

Avoid phrases like:

- “I think…”
- “Maybe…”
- “I guess…”
- “You can just…”
- “I don’t know…”

Use stronger language:

- “Absolutely.”
- “The best next step is…”
- “Here’s what we can do…”
- “Let’s get that checked properly.”

Customers call us for confidence. Give it to them.

Today’s challenge: replace weak language with confident guidance.

---

## Thursday Focus: Confirm the Next Step

Team, today’s focus is appointment and next-step confirmation.

Every customer should know:

1. When they are coming in.
2. What we are checking.
3. What happens next.
4. How we will communicate.

Use this close:

“Perfect. I have you scheduled for [day/time]. When you arrive, we’ll confirm your concern, perform the inspection, and call you with clear next steps before any work is done. Is this the best number to reach you?”

Today’s challenge: no call ends without a clear next step.

---

## Friday Focus: Finish With Confidence

Team, today we finish strong.

End the call like a professional:

“Thank you, we’ll see you then.”

Simple. Confident. Clear.

The customer should hang up feeling like they made the right decision.

This month’s standard is simple:

Start professionally. Listen patiently. Speak with clarity. Close with direction.

Today’s challenge: finish every call with gratitude and certainty.

That is Automotive Hospitality. Let’s cook.
